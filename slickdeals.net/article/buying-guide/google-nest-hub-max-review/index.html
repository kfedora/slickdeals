Finally, We Can Watch Netflix on Google’s Nest Hub Devices!

Bringing more entertainment to kitchens and dining rooms everywhere!

Mike Jackson July 21, 2020
8 minute read
Advertiser Disclosure: At Slickdeals, we work hard to help you find the best deals. To do this, some of the products featured here are from our partners, who may provide us with compensation. However, this doesn’t influence our opinions. Our views are our own.

After what feels like a lifetime of waiting, Google’s Nest Hub and Nest Hub Max are now the first-ever smart displays to offer Netflix support, a feature that has been at the top of many users’ wishlists (or complaints lists).

Starting today, Nest smart displays around the world will be receiving updates that allow you to ask for Netflix titles with just your voice. Say, “Hey Google, play Queer Eye” to instantly watch the latest episode, or say, “Hey Google, open Netflix” and browse through a curated list of shows and movies.

And because most people have Nest Hub displays in the kitchen or dining room, where hands might be dirty or busy at any given moment, you can simply ask Netflix (or the Google Assistant, rather) to pause, skip forward or rewind. On the Nest Hub Max, Netflix will respond to Quick Gestures as well, making the experience truly hands-free.

The addition of Netflix is just one of the latest quality-of-life improvements for Google’s Nest Hub lineup. Keep scrolling for our hands-on review of the Google Nest Hub Max, and learn how it can complement any lifestyle or home.

Review: Google’s Nest Hub Max is Focused, Refined and Powerful

If you’re creating a smart home setup around Google’s ecosystem, this is the control center you need.

*****

Nobody really needs a Google Nest Hub Max, but it’s one of those devices that works its way into your life in such a way that you then find it hard to live without.

It serves not only as the central control hub for all of your interconnected smart home devices in the Google ecosystem, but also as a surprisingly multi-talented assistant to your daily life.

Google aficionados will recognize the Nest Hub Max as a bigger sibling to the original Nest Hub, but it adds much more than just a larger screen. A new camera and the increased space for larger, meatier speakers (including a subwoofer!) adds to the entire user experience in just about every way.

The Nest Hub Max represents the premium end of Google’s smart home offerings, and while it does a lot of things well, it may not be the perfect device for everyone. Let’s take a closer look at how the Nest Hub Max might fit into your lifestyle.

Google Nest Hub Max Technical Specifications

  • 10-inch touch screen display (1200×800 resolution)
  • Dual far-field microphones
  • 6.5MP front-facing camera
  • Three speaker array (two 10W tweeters, one 30W subwoofer)
  • Ambient light sensor
  • 2.4GHz / 5GHz Wi-Fi
  • Bluetooth 5.0
  • Google Assistant built-in
  • Chromecast built-in

A Minimalist Design that Blends into Your Home

The Nest Hub Max is a clean, simple device to look at. A 10-inch display with a front-facing camera sits atop a grey felt-covered base containing some surprisingly hefty-sounding speakers. The only buttons are a volume rocker and switch for deactivating the camera, and that’s it.

Setup is also easy – you plug it in, and it springs to life, immediately guiding you through the process using on-screen prompts for connecting with your Wi-Fi and the Google Home app on your smartphone. Within 10 minutes it’ll be ready and waiting to receive your commands.

For better or for worse, it’s a minimalist design in almost every way. With almost no buttons, no complex cluster of on-board apps and no app store to download new programs, the voice-activated Google Assistant sits at the core of the highly focused Nest Hub experience.

And the hardware serves this purpose excellently. The dual far-field mics can recognize your commands reliably from across a large room, even amidst loud ambient noise (like music). The screen is bright and crisp, with auto-dimming handled via an ambient light sensor in the bezel. And the device sports an overall pleasant yet neutral aesthetic that blends effortlessly into your home.

Those with special privacy concerns may be conscientious of the lack of a physical camera shield, but software and hardware deactivation is present, and clearly communicated via an LED status light.

Your Family’s Personal Assistant

Most Google users are familiar with the power of Google Assistant, and the Nest Hub Max adds a new layer to how you can engage with your personal secretary. Asking for the weather in your area or the traffic on your route to work is barely scratching the surface.

Advanced actions include tracking and adding to your list of reminders and events on your personal calendar. You can also ask it to add entries to a shopping list or even order items for you from major stores like Walmart or Target.

It’ll very quickly become an essential tool in your kitchen. Your virtual sous chef can start timers, convert measurements or even walk you through full recipes, which is super handy to do with voice commands when you’re busy with food prep or washing dishes.

Sure, any Google Assistant-equipped device can do this stuff, but the Nest Hub Max greatly enhances the experience with its large screen and robust speakers.

There’s a plethora of useful things that the Google Assistant can do, and you can learn more about them here.

Convenient Multimedia Delivery

The Nest Hub Max’s large 10-inch screen brings a visualization to your interactions that proves invaluable. All of the information it feeds you benefits from on-screen embellishment.

You won’t have to trust that it’s timing your eggs — you’ll see the timer on-screen. Weather updates are shown with graphics and extra details. Traffic reports come paired with a map of the route. Need help with a recipe, or a DIY project? Ask it to show you cooking instructions or a tutorial video from YouTube. The presence of that screen enhances the entire experience.

Bigger hardware also means bigger speakers, and music sounds excellent on this impressive setup, complete with a rear subwoofer. You can link to YouTube Music, Spotify, Pandora and more to cater to your musical demands, and it’s capable of filling a room with deep, bass-y audio.

Video looks and sounds great, too. Just tell Google to play a title that’s available on one of your connected services like Netflix, YouTube TV, Hulu, HBO, CBS All Access or Google Play Movies and it will instantly comply. And you can even use the Hub as a digital photo frame, pulling pictures from your shared Google Photo libraries or curated lists from Google.

The streamlined nature of the Nest Hub Max does, however, translate into some significant limitations with media content. There’s no web browser to visit full media websites, and searching for shows on Hulu is not possible. It will only play your most recent episode of a specific show.

YouTube support is equally as limited — browsing playlists, categories or user comments isn’t possible. There’s also no way to like or subscribe to videos or channels. Essentially, you’re limited to watching a specific video that you asked for, although you can always cast a YouTube video from your smartphone or tablet to the Nest Hub Max.

If you were expecting the Nest Hub Max to basically be a tablet with large speakers, I’m sorry to inform you that it isn’t one. Instead, it’s a nice stop-gap for when you’re away from your main TV or computer, but it doesn’t quite stand on its own as a primary viewing device — think of it more as a friendly face of the Google Assistant.

An Eye for Innovation

Another major upgrade over the original Nest Hub is the addition of that 6.5 megapixel camera, which adds a whole host of capabilities.

Hand gestures are an obvious benefit — for example, you can pause media by holding out your hand — but they’re limited in use and practicality.

By far the most interesting feature is facial recognition. When you approach the Nest Hub Max it will recognize you and instantly pull up information and content that’s personalized for you.

This, along with its voice recognition capabilities, means that multiple people in the house can get the best out of the Nest Hub Max without affecting each others’ data or accounts. If your partner creates a new calendar entry, only they will see it and it’ll only be on their Google account. If you ask for the best route to work, it will access the correct destination. You can even tell Google Assistant to set a reminder for another person, and they will see it when they approach the Nest Hub Max.

The built-in camera also helps the Nest Hub Max double up as a home security camera, with full integration via the Nest mobile app and Nest Aware video recording service. It lacks night vision, but is fully compatible with Nest Aware security features like motion detection and event monitoring, along with a live feed accessible at any time. This is excellent for those who already use Nest cameras for home security — you’ve suddenly got more security coverage with the Nest Hub Max.

Of course, the camera makes video calling possible, using contacts and groups set up in the Google Duo app (or the more business-oriented Google Meets, if you prefer). With a large screen, clear audio, sensitive microphones and the ability to intelligently track your movements around the room, video conversations work well… when you can find someone to chat with.

And that brings me to my next point: I wouldn’t necessarily recommend the Nest Hub Max for those who are brand agnostic and simply want a dedicated video chat device because Google Duo lacks the near-ubiquitous adoption of other platforms like Facebook Messaging or Apple FaceTime. Although my contacts list has a pretty even split between iPhone and Android users, I struggled to find someone to call via Google Duo. If I had Google Fi, a low-cost stand-alone cell service, it’d be a completely different story — the Nest Hub Max would then become an extension of my cell phone thanks to Google Fi’s Wi-Fi calling capabilities.

Platform adoption aside, video calling is a nice addition to this already-capable smart home assistant, and inviting users to Google Duo is quick and easy, especially because it comes pre-installed on most new Android phones.

The Brains for Your Smart Home

One of the best things about having a smart home assistant, like the Nest Hub Max, is the ability to control other devices in your home. Google Assistance boasts some of the best integration with smart devices including lights, thermostats, fans, security cameras, speakers and lots more.

Simply blurting ‘on’ and ‘off’ commands is great, but fails to realize the full potential of this capability. Google utilizes a feature called Routines, which lets you assign a list of actions to a single command.

For example, the “Good Morning” routine is fantastic. Say “Hey Google, Good Morning” as you roll out of bed and it’ll greet you, tell you the time and the weather, offer an overview of your reminders and appointments for the day, and then play the latest news report video via Reuters (or the news source of your choice).

Routines are fully customizable, making them incredibly powerful. You can, for example, set one up to turn on your lights and ceiling fan, lower your A/C, deactivate your security cameras and play some relaxing music when you arrive home, or dim the lights and silence your phone when it’s time to watch a movie. This is one of those features that rewards the effort you put into it, and becomes more integral to your life as time passes.

Should You Buy the Google Nest Hub Max?

I can’t help but emphasize that the Google Nest Hub Max is not a tablet device. This is important to understand. Don’t buy it just to watch YouTube, to use as a portable TV, or as a dedicated video calling device. You should get a proper tablet with full app store capabilities to do that.

In actuality, the Google Nest Hub Max is a focused smart home hub, and one of the best on the market today. It uses its 10-inch screen, interactive camera and robust speakers to enhance just about every aspect of the smart home assistant experience. It’s well beyond what you’d get with a typical “smart speaker,” justifying the $229 price tag, and integrating itself even more deeply into your life as a result.

The hardware itself is sleek, modern and beautiful. Google Assistant is intelligent and powerful. And most importantly, Google’s network of smart device integration is, at this point, broad and well established, meaning your shopping choices for additional smart devices is almost limitless.

If your home already relies on a variety of connected devices, then the Nest Hub Max is the key element for ease of access and coordination. And if you’re just starting down the road to a smart home, you can feel confident with the knowledge that Google’s smart device ecosystem is vast and mature, ensuring a long lifespan and lasting support for the Nest Hub Max.

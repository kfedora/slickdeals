What Are Bank Account Bonuses?

Many banks and credit unions offer introductory bonuses to incentivize people to sign up for an account. Depending on the financial institution, we’ve seen bank account bonuses ranging from $50 to $1,000. They’re often offered through limited-time promotions, both for checking accounts and savings accounts, and the requirements to earn one can vary. Here are some of the things you may have to do to earn one:

  • Spend a certain amount on your debit card within a certain period.
  • Sign up for direct deposit and meet a minimum deposit requirement.
  • Deposit a minimum amount of cash and hold it there for a set timeframe.
  • Make a minimum number of bill payments from your account within a set period.

Because there are so many different ways to earn a bank account bonus, you can pick and choose the accounts that you can earn easily. Plus, there’s usually no hard inquiry on your credit report like you get when you apply for a credit card. Instead, banks typically only require a soft pull on your credit report. However, bank accounts do get reported to ChexSystems, and if your ChexSystems report shows too many recent accounts, you may get denied.

Additionally, many banks may only allow you to receive one new checking account-related bonus per calendar year.

How to Avoid Fees

Monthly fees aren’t common with credit unions, but they’re pretty standard with major banks. As you compare bonus offers, look at the account’s fee structure to determine how much you’ll actually earn. For example, you may get a $100 bonus on an account with a $12 monthly fee. What’s more, if you close the account within six months, you’ll forfeit your bonus.

If you can’t find a way to get that monthly fee waived — which is often possible — your actual earnings from the bonus minus the monthly fees are just $28.

Also, remember that bank account bonuses are taxable as interest-bearing income. So remember to watch out for 1099-INT forms when you get ready to file your tax return.

There is no minimum deposit required; all you have to do is open your account and set up direct deposit and make your first deposit within 90 days. You’ll then receive $300 in your account within 10 business days. The direct deposit must be an electronic deposit of your paycheck, government benefits like Social Security or a pension from your employer or the government.

There is a $25 monthly service fee unless you do one of the following each monthly statement period: maintain an average beginning day balance of $15,000+ in combination of your account and linked qualifying Chase checking account, savings account and other balances or link a qualifying Chase first mortgage and enroll in automatic payments from your Chase account.

You will earn interest on this account, and you won’t get charged fees for Chase design checks when you order them through Chase or your first four non-Chase ATM transactions per statement period. You can set up your account online at Chase’s website or in-branch. Applying for an account through Chase is a soft pull on your credit. Access your through their website or 24/7 with the Chase Mobile app.

New Pixar Movie ‘Soul’ Will Release on Disney+, but is it Worth it?

Do exclusive releases make Disney+ worthwhile?

Quan Tsang October 8, 2020
19 minute read
Advertiser Disclosure: At Slickdeals, we work hard to help you find the best deals. To do this, some of the products featured here are from our partners, who may provide us with compensation. However, this doesn’t influence our opinions. Our views are our own.

Originally planned for theatrical release in late November, Disney has announced that it will now debut Soul (starring Jamie Foxx) on streaming service Disney+ on Christmas Day, December 25. So far, it seems that Soul will be available to Disney+ users as part of the regular streaming service, as opposed to the live-action reboot of Mulan which costs $29.99 in addition to subscription costs.

There are still no free trials for Disney+ (days before in-Manuel Miranda’s Hamilton musical hit Disney+ on Friday, July 3rd, Disney abruptly ended its free trial program), so if you want to see Soul and you’re not currently a subscriber, you’ll have to sign up for the service starting at $6.99 per month.

Subscribe to Disney+

This belated release is somewhat at odds with other Disney+ movies like Onward, Frozen II and Star Wars Episode IX: The Rise of Skywalker, all of which came to the service ahead of schedule earlier this year.

If you’d like more information on Disney+, check out our full writeup of the service below:

While Disney isn’t currently offering a discount on standalone Disney+ subscriptions, you can always opt for the bundle that comes with Disney+, Hulu and ESPN+. As long as you plan on using more than one of these services, you’ll be getting them for less than their standalone prices.

Subscribe to Disney+

Disney+ officially launched November 12th, 2019, and we’re happy to report the new service successfully established partnerships with most of the biggest streaming platforms in time for launch day.

 

 

Everything Coming to Disney+ in September

Disney+ came straight out the gate in 2019 with a near-encyclopedic selection of the entertainment company’s creations. One minute, you could watch any of your favorite Pixar classics, and the next you could indulge your nostalgia with a Disney Channel original movie.

Since living rooms have become the new movie theaters, Disney+ is a great way to bring your favorite entertainment straight to your TV. In addition to the entire Stars Wars collection, from New Hope to The Rise of Skywalker, you can watch other classic family films. Has it been a while since you watched Bend it Like Beckham or D2: The Mighty Ducks? These heartwarming films can be streamed in August.

To our delight, even with over 100 classic movies and shows to offer, the Disney+ catalog isn’t complete yet. As the months roll on, the company plans to add more content to the platform.

The first months of Disney+ has seen much success, so plenty of people are interested in knowing everything coming to Disney+ in September. Based on what’s to come, you can expect not only great things this month but also into the fall as well.

How Much Does Disney+ Cost? 

Since we love a good bargain here at Slickdeals, the price for Disney Plus gives us another reason to be excited. The streaming service costs $6.99 a month or $69.99 ($5.83 a month) if you pay for the year. This pricing strategy is definitely an intentional jab to Netflix, which costs almost twice as much, and raised its prices yet again in 2019. While a seven-day free trial was available at launch, Disney pulled this offer just before the release of Hamilton in July 2020. We’re hoping another free trial pops up once the current hype dies down some.

To continue with the discounts, Disney also announced a streaming bundle that includes its own service as well as ESPN Plus and the ad-supported version of Hulu. The bundle, however, will not be avilable until launch day. The combo deal will be offered at $12.99, is $5 less expensive than subscribing to each service separately. With this price, the package sits right at the same price as Netflix’s most popular plan. It seems like a clear elbow to the current streaming champion, but Disney’s CEO claims it’s a mere coincidence.

If you are waiting for a sale, Disney has been known to offer good discounts on long-term commitments to Disney+. A few months before the streaming service launched, the company offered a three-year subscription plan for $141 (or roughly $47 a year), which is nearly $70 less than retail price. Then, on Cyber Monday 2019, a one-year subscription to Disney+ went on sale for $59.99, or $10 off. If your patience holds out, you might be able to find a better deal once the service has been out longer.

Which Devices Support Disney+

Disney successfully established deals with the majority of well-known streaming platforms prior to its launch. If you’re currently using other streaming services, chances are good you’ll be able to watch Disney+ no problem.

Additionally, certain devices have further compatibility with Disney+, like Google-Assistant enabled Smart Displays. If you have a Nest Hub or Nest Hub Max along with your Disney+ subscription, now you can simply link the two in the Google Home app. Then, you can give a verbal command to your Nest Hub like “Hey Google, play Avengers: Endgame on Disney+” and your device will start streaming the show!

Hulu, ESPN+ and Disney+ Bundle

Disney previously had a minor stake in Hulu, but, with the acquisition of Fox, the company now holds some major control. But rather than assimilate the streaming service, it looks like Disney want to piggyback on the Netflix competitor instead. If you’re interested in subscribing to both Disney+ and Hulu, Disney is offering a bundle package that includes both services as well as ESPN+, all for $12.99 per month (a 25% savings).

Subscribe to the Disney+ / Hulu / ESPN+ Bundle

What’s Included in the Disney+ bundle?
While this offer gets you ESPN+, Hulu and Disney+, it may not be right for everyone. Both ESPN+ and Hulu feature commercials when purchased through this bundle, whereas you can pay a premium to get commercial-free versions of the services on their own. If you can’t stand the thought of your media being interrupted, you may not be totally satisfied with the versions of Hulu and ESPN+ you get with this package. However, anyone who plans to use all three, and who doesn’t mind the occasional 30-second ad, should totally consider purchasing the bundle direct from Disney.

How Many People Can Use One Disney+ Account?

Like Netflix, Disney+ lets you create multiple user profiles for one account. However, Disney has upped the ante by allowing up to seven profiles, as opposed to Netflix’s five. On top of this, you can stream Disney+ to up to four devices at once, so families of all sizes (and distances) should have no problem sharing a single subscription.

Titles Currently Available on Disney+

In the long run, Disney’s streaming service could actually end up saving you some money. The execs stated that new films will arrive on the platform directly after their home release. In fact, Kevin Feige, President of Marvel Studios, shared that Captain Marvel and Avengers: Endgame will make their streaming debuts exclusively on the service. However, Endgame won’t be on the platform until December 11th, according to Disney.

Despite being a little late to the game, the entertainment behemoth has been amassing a content arsenal that could really shake up the streaming industry. With Pixar, Star Wars, Marvel and now 21t Century Fox under the hood, Disney may drive a significant chunk of the market to its service. This is especially true if it starts pulling Disney content from competitors Netflix and Amazon Prime Video.

But even if Disney continues to share its content with other services, the slated releases for the new platform are definite attention-grabbers. The list is long, but there are a few worth noting.

Up first is Loki, a new Marvel Cinematic Universe TV show with the ever-charming Tom Hiddleston reprising his role as the God of Mischief. If you are a Monsters Inc. fan, Mike Wazowski and Sulley are also making a comeback with a new animated series called Monsters at Work.

And if the force be with you, Jon Favreau presents us with The Mandalorian, an eight-episode Star Wars spin-off following the “travails of a lone gunfighter in the outer reaches of the galaxy.”

This summer also brings us some more updates on upcoming projects for Disney+. Taking in Fox brought the X-Men and Deadpool home to the MCU, but there are a number of Fox classic movies due for a makeover. And if there is any company adept at doing reboots, it’s Disney. The company has hinted at remakes for franchises like Home Alone, Night at the Museum and Diary of a Wimpy Kid.
